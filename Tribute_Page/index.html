Nikola Tesla

The man who change the world

Here's a time line of Nikola Tesla Investions

  • Born: July 10, 1856, Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia)
  • 1882 – The Rotating Magnetic Field
    • Discovered the rotating magnetic field, the basis for AC (alternating current) motors.
  • 1887 – AC Induction Motor & Polyphase System
    • Invented the induction motor, which became essential in modern electrical engineering.
    • Developed the polyphase AC system used in power grids today.
  • 1891 – The Tesla Coil
    • Invented the Tesla Coil, a high-voltage transformer for wireless energy transmission.
    • Still used in radio technology and experiments today.
  • 1893 – Wireless Lighting & AC Power at the Chicago World’s Fair
    • Demonstrated **wireless lighting** using induction at the Chicago World’s Fair.
    • Proved **AC power’s superiority** over Edison’s DC system.
  • 1895 – Niagara Falls Hydroelectric Plant
    • Designed the **first hydroelectric power plant** at **Niagara Falls**.
    • Provided **power transmission over long distances**, setting the standard for modern power grids.
  • 1898 – Radio-Controlled Boat
    • Demonstrated **wireless remote control** with a radio-controlled boat at Madison Square Garden.
    • Considered the foundation of **modern robotics and remote-controlled technology**.
  • 1901-1905 – Wardenclyffe Tower (Wireless Power Transmission)
    • Began construction of **Wardenclyffe Tower**, aiming to transmit **wireless electricity** worldwide.
    • The project was abandoned in 1905 due to financial issues.
  • 1917 – Radar Concept
    • Proposed the idea of **radar technology** to the U.S. military.
    • The concept was ignored at the time but later developed during **World War II**.
  • January 7, 1943: Dies in New York City, USA
